Should I opt for the new tax regime or stick with the old one?

Expert Answered
Taxation Income Tax

I'm confused about whether to opt for the new tax regime New Tax Regime
Introduced in Budget 2020 and modified in subsequent budgets, the new tax regime offers lower tax rates but removes most exemptions and deductions available under the old regime.

Example: Under the new regime, you might pay taxes at lower rates (5%, 10%, 15%, etc.) but cannot claim deductions like HRA, Section 80C investments, or home loan interest. or stick with the old tax regime Old Tax Regime
The traditional tax structure with higher base tax rates but multiple exemptions and deductions that can reduce taxable income.

Example: Under the old regime, you can claim deductions like ₹1.5 lakhs under Section 80C for investments in PPF, ELSS, etc., and additional deductions for health insurance, home loan interest, and more. . My annual salary is ₹12 lakhs, and I invest in PPF, ELSS funds, and pay a home loan EMI. Which regime would be better for me?

Raj Maha

Tax Expert

Based on your situation with home loan interest and investments in tax-saving instruments, the old regime is likely more beneficial for you. With a ₹12 lakh salary, your deductions (80C, home loan, etc.) probably exceed ₹3 lakhs, making the old regime more tax-efficient despite higher base rates.
