SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)

Investing

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) Systematic Investment Plan (SIP)
An investment method where investors make regular, equal payments into a mutual fund, rather than investing a large sum at once.

Key Benefits: Rupee-cost averaging, disciplined investing, power of compounding. is a method of investing a fixed amount regularly in mutual funds, typically monthly or quarterly. This enables investors to benefit from rupee-cost averaging Rupee-Cost Averaging
A strategy where you invest a fixed amount at regular intervals, regardless of share price - buying more units when prices are low and fewer when prices are high.

Example: Investing ₹5,000 monthly will buy more mutual fund units when markets are down and fewer when markets are up. and the power of compounding Power of Compounding
The process where the return on your investment generates additional earnings over time, which themselves generate returns.

Example: An investment of ₹1,000 monthly for 20 years at 12% returns could grow to approximately ₹95 lakhs. over time.

Example in Action:

Priya starts a ₹5,000 monthly SIP in an equity mutual fund with a historical return of 12% p.a. After 10 years, her total investment of ₹6 lakhs (₹5,000 × 12 months × 10 years) grows to approximately ₹11.6 lakhs, with an additional ₹5.6 lakhs generated through compounding.
