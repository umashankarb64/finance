MYTH

"You need a lot of money to start investing in the stock market"

Investing Stock Market Trending

THE CLAIM:

"You need at least ₹50,000 or more to start investing in the stock market. Small investments are not worth it because of high brokerage fees and minimal returns."

THE FACT:

You can start investing in the Indian stock market with as little as ₹500. Many discount brokers Discount Brokers
Brokerage firms that execute trades at significantly lower commissions compared to full-service brokers, typically without providing investment advice or research.

Example: Zerodha, Upstox, and Groww are popular discount brokers in India that charge flat fees per trade rather than percentage-based fees. now offer zero brokerage for equity delivery, and platforms allow investments in fractional shares Fractional Shares
Portions of a single share of a company, allowing investors to buy a slice of high-priced stocks with smaller amounts of money.

Example: Instead of buying one full share of Amazon at ₹30,000, you could invest ₹5,000 to own 1/6th of a share. and mutual fund SIPs Mutual Fund SIPs (Systematic Investment Plans)
An investment method that allows investors to invest fixed amounts regularly in mutual funds, typically monthly or quarterly.

Example: Setting up a monthly SIP of ₹500 in an index fund that invests in Nifty 50 companies. with very small amounts.

The power of compounding Compounding
The process by which an investment generates earnings not only on the initial principal but also on accumulated earnings from previous periods.

Example: A monthly investment of ₹1,000 at 12% annual returns can grow to over ₹4.5 lakhs in 10 years due to compounding. works even on small investments. Starting early with small amounts is often more beneficial than waiting to accumulate a large sum. According to SEBI data, over 70% of new investors in 2023-24 started with investments under ₹10,000.

SOURCES & EVIDENCE:

SEBI Annual Report 2023-24 NSE Investor Survey 2025 AMFI Data on Small SIPs

MYTH

"Credit cards always lead to debt problems"

Banking Personal Finance

THE CLAIM:

"Credit cards are debt traps designed to make you spend more than you can afford. They always lead to financial problems and high-interest debt."

THE FACT:

When used responsibly, credit cards can be valuable financial tools that offer several benefits. The key is to pay the full statement balance by the due date to avoid interest charges Credit Card Interest Charges
Fees charged on unpaid credit card balances, typically calculated as a percentage of the outstanding amount and added to your balance each month.

Example: If your credit card has a 36% annual interest rate and you carry a balance of ₹10,000, you'll pay approximately ₹300 in interest each month. . Benefits include:

  • Building a positive credit history Credit History
    A record of how you've managed credit accounts over time, including payment history, credit utilization, and account longevity.

    Example: Making on-time credit card payments that are reported to credit bureaus like CIBIL, building a positive track record that can help secure future loans.     and improving your credit score Credit Score
    A numerical representation of your creditworthiness based on your credit history, typically ranging from 300 to 900 in India.

    Example: A CIBIL score of 750+ is generally considered good and may help you secure loans at better interest rates.
  • Reward points, cashback, and discounts on purchases
  • Additional protections like purchase protection, extended warranties, and travel insurance
  • Improved expense tracking through monthly statements
  • Float period of up to 45-50 days of interest-free credit

According to RBI data, about 35% of credit card users in India pay their bills in full every month, demonstrating that responsible usage is possible and common.

SOURCES & EVIDENCE:

RBI Credit Card Statistics 2024 TransUnion CIBIL Report

FACT CHECK

"Bitcoin will always increase in value over time"

Cryptocurrency Investing Trending

THE CLAIM:

"Bitcoin is designed to always increase in value over time due to its limited supply of 21 million coins. This scarcity guarantees that it will be worth much more in the future."

THE FACT:

While Bitcoin's limited supply of 21 million coins does create scarcity Scarcity
The limited availability of a resource or asset, which can potentially increase its value when demand exceeds supply.

Example: Bitcoin's maximum supply is capped at 21 million coins, creating built-in scarcity unlike traditional currencies that can be printed indefinitely. , this alone does not guarantee increasing value. Bitcoin's price is determined by multiple factors:

  • Market demand and adoption rates
  • Regulatory developments across different countries
  • Technological advancements and competition from other cryptocurrencies
  • Macroeconomic factors and investor sentiment

Bitcoin has experienced significant volatility Volatility
The degree of variation in trading prices over time, indicating the risk associated with an asset. Higher volatility means more dramatic price swings.

Example: Bitcoin's price dropping from nearly ₹50 lakhs to under ₹20 lakhs within a few months in 2021-2022, showing extreme price volatility. throughout its history. For example, after reaching nearly $69,000 in November 2021, Bitcoin's value dropped below $17,000 in 2022 – a decline of over 75%. While it has recovered since then, such patterns demonstrate that Bitcoin can experience prolonged periods of decreasing value.

Historic performance is not indicative of future results, and all investments carry risk. Cryptocurrency investments are particularly speculative and can experience significant losses as well as gains.
